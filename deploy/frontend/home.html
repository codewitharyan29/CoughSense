The Problem

Access to respiratory screening is limited in low-resource settings — clinics are far, tests cost money, results take time. Yet a cough carries information clinicians already listen for: wet vs. dry, productive vs. barking. If a machine can learn those same acoustic patterns, screening becomes as accessible as a phone call.

Our Approach
  • Feature extraction — MFCCs, spectral features for classical ML; log-mel spectrograms for deep learning.
  • Three models, compared honestly — Random Forest, XGBoost, and a compact CNN.
  • Explainable — SHAP analysis shows exactly which acoustic features drive each prediction.
  • Rigorously evaluated — every number is 5-fold cross-validated, reported with its variance.
Why It Matters
  • Underexplored modality — audio biomarkers get far less attention than image or text ML.
  • Real-world value — instant pre-screening anywhere a phone works.
  • Transparent, not a black box — decisions are explainable and clinically grounded.
  • Honest science — limitations are documented, not hidden.
A Real Experiment: Quality vs. Quantity

We tested scaling the dataset from 121 clinical clips to 321 using crowdsourced COUGHVID data. Accuracy dropped from 85% to ~65% — because self-reported labels are far noisier than lab-confirmed ones. We kept the clean clinical data instead.
