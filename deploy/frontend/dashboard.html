Experiment — Data Quality vs Quantity
We tested whether adding more data helps. Scaling from 121 clinical clips to 321 by adding crowdsourced COUGHVID recordings dropped accuracy from 85% to 65% — because self-reported crowdsourced labels are far noisier than lab-confirmed clinical ones. Quality filters (cough-detection ≥ 0.8, SNR sorting) recovered only a few points.

Lesson: in medical ML, data quality beats quantity. We also tested a Random-Forest + XGBoost ensemble and an enriched 102-feature set (delta-MFCCs, spectral contrast) — neither beat the simple 37-feature XGBoost, confirming the final model is already well-matched to the problem.
